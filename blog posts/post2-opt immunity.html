6 Food Items For Optimizing Your Immunity

The Immune system protects the body against infections, viruses and other external and internal insults by utilizing three distinct layers i.e, physical and chemical barriers, nonspecific resistance, and specific resistance depending on the nature of the threat. Immunity in a human body depends vastly on the age of the particular, a newly born baby tends to have low immunity which gets better as he ages and gets optimal in adolescents and adults and starts to gradually decline as he ages.


Diet is one of the most important factors that affect immune function in a Human body along with other environmental factors such as ageing, stress etc. Adequate nutrition is crucial to ensure a good supply of the energy sources, macronutrients and micronutrients required for the development and maintenance of the immune response. Micronutrients have vital roles throughout the immune system that are independent of the age of a particular person and it has been determined that those most needed for a healthy immune system include:



  • Vitamin A
  • Vitamin C
  • Vitamin D
  • Vitamin E
  • Vitamin B6
  • Vitamin B12
  • Folic acid/Folate
  • Zinc

    • Following are some of the most important food items that are rich in these vital nutrients which help in keeping your body safe from any viral or bacterial infections.


    1. Kiwi fruit


      • One of the highest sources of vitamin C found in any food.
      • One of the highest sources of vitamin E among other fruits.
      • 1 medium-sized fruit is enough to fulfil an adult's daily vitamin C requirement*
      • Other than just helping build a strong immune system it also helps in keeping your skin, bones and tissue healthy.
      • Vitamin C tends to get less effective when heated thus it being a fruit makes it possible to consume it raw and get the most vitamin C out of it.

    2. Broccoli


      • One of the highest sources of vitamin C found in any food
      • 100 gms of Broccoli is enough to fulfil an adult's daily requirement of Vitamin C.
      • They are high in protein which helps build those essential muscles.
      • Also helps in keeping your skin and bones healthy
      • Eating a little less of some food items

    3. Leafy Green Vegetables


      • 200 gms of the leafy vegetables such as Spinach and Turnip greens each have enough Vitamin A and C required for your body daily.
      • Another leafy vegetable, Kale also has a good amount of Vitamin C and A.
      • It also helps with better eye vision and healthier skin.
      • Turnip greens also have a good amount of Vitamin E.

    4. Fish


      • Fishes such as Salmon, Mackarel and Herring are good sources of Vitamin D for your body especially important for those who lack proper exposure to enough sunlight as sunlight itself is a good source of Vitamin D.
      • They are also a good source of vitamin B12 which are abundant in many foods.
      • 100 gms of any of these fish is more than enough for an adult's daily requirement of Vitamin B12.

    5. Seeds


      • Seeds such as Sunflower seeds are one of the richest sources of Vitamin E, Folate, Zinc and Iron.
      • 50 gms of sunflower seeds are enough for fulfilling the daily requirements of Vitamin E in an adult.
      • Sunflower seeds can be called as a master food as it has almost all of the nutrients in a good proportion required for healthy immune functioning.
      • Seeds of Sesame are a good source of Iron and Zinc.
      • A portion of 100 gms of sesame seeds is enough for an adult's daily requirement of Iron and Zinc.

    6. Liver


      • Both Chicken and Beef livers are the richest sources of Vitamin A, Vitamin B6, Vitamin B12, Folate and Iron.
      • 100 gms of any of these Liver is enough to fulfil an adult's daily requirement for Vitamin A, B6, B12, Folate and Iron.

    Things to avoid:


    Drinking a lot of alcohol, It is proven that drinking a small amount of alcohol is good for your health but going all out of control will harm your immune functionality thus resulting in bad health.


    Important Facts:


    • Vitamin C is important for keeping your skin, bones and connective tissue healthy
    • Vitamin C is important in helping you absorb iron from your food
    • Cutting and heating foods change vitamin C and make it less effective
    • Your body needs vitamin D to absorb calcium properly and keep your bones and muscles working properly
    • We get most of the vitamin D we need from sunlight.
    • The best food sources of vitamin D are fatty fish like salmon, herring and mackerel, and eggs
    • The amount of vitamin D you need varies with your age
    • Vitamin E is important for the proper functioning of many of the body's organs.
    • Taking very large doses of vitamin E can be harmful, especially to people on blood-thinning medicines such as warfarin and some cholesterol medicines.
    • Vitamin B12 is essential for your blood, brain and nervous system.
    • Not having enough Vitamin B12 in your body can make you feel tired and sick.
    • Vegetarians can get enough vitamin B12 from dairy foods and eggs.
    • The only way for vegans to get enough is by eating foods fortified with vitamin B12 or by taking a supplement.
    • Zinc supports normal growth and development during pregnancy.
    • Women need more iron about twice as much as men to replace the amount lost in blood during menstruation.
    • How you prepare food, and which foods you eat together, can affect how much iron your body absorbs.
    • Coffee, tea and red wine (both alcoholic and non-alcoholic), on the other hand, can reduce iron absorption.
    • Frozen is fine. Manufacturers freeze frozen fruits and veggies at “peak” ripeness, which means they’ll pack a similar nutritional value as their fresh counterparts.
